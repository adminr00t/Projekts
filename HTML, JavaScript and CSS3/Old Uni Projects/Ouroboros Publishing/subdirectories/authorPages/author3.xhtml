Max Brooks

Biography

  • Born on the 22nd of May, 1972, Max Brooks would start writing as a member of the writing team for Saturday Night Live from 2001 to 2003. In 2006, he then published World War Z, which would later be developed into a movie. Max claimed that the main inspiration for World War Z was The Good War: An Oral History of World War 2, stating "I read it when I was a teenager and its sat with me ever since".



Works

  1. World War Z- Narrated by a UN Postwar Commision agent, this novel is a collection of individual accounts following the devastating global war against a zombie plaque.

  2. Devolution - After recovering the journals of Kate Holland, it is soon revealed that a tragic disaster, cutting a town off from society, awakened a creature that was once thought impossible to exist

  3. The Zombie Survival Guide - This is a manual detailing how to deal with the potentiality of a zombie outbreak, containing detailed plans of what to do.

  4. The Harlem Hellfighters - A graphic novel following a fictionalized account of the largely African-American 369th Infantry Regiment, who are nicknamed "Hell-Fighters" by german soldiers during the first world war
